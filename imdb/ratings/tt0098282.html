Storyline

A man is murdered. Two men witness it. A blind man who hears the killer, and a deaf man who sees them. The police don't think they're credible witnesses, but the killers don't want to take any chances. The two men must now work together to save themselves and bring the killers to justice. Written by Jean-Marc Rocher <tss.yh.nec.co.jp>

Did You Know?

Trivia

Third of four star teamings of actors Richard Pryor and Gene Wilder. The other movies were Zwei wahnsinnig starke Typen (1980), Das andere Ich (1991) and Trans-Amerika-Express (1976). Additionally, both men were involved in Der wilde wilde Westen (1974): Pryor as a co-writer and Gene as Jim, the Waco Kid. See more »

User Reviews

 
The funniest Wilder/Pryor movie
22 January 2002 | by DragoonKain (New York) – See all my reviews

Just saw this movie again recently, and it still gets me laughing like crazy. This was the first Pryor/Wilder movie I ever saw, when it first came out on video, and it eventually got me to the video store several more times to see their other movies. Although all of them got me laughing (especially Silver Streak), this one was by far the funniest. It's definitely an underrated movie. I only wish Pryor and Wilder had done more together.
